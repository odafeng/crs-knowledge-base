Encorafenib, Cetuximab and mFOLFOX6 in BRAF-mutant Colorectal Cancer

Authors: Élez E, Tabernero J, Kopetz S, et al.
Journal: N Engl J Med 2025;392:2425–2437
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2501912
PMID: 40444708 | PMC: PMC12197837
Source: Retrieved from PubMed Central (PMC)

Abstract

BACKGROUND: The open-label, phase 3 BREAKWATER study evaluated first-line encorafenib plus cetuximab with or without chemotherapy (oxaliplatin, leucovorin, and 5-FU) (EC±mFOLFOX6) vs. standard of care (SOC; chemotherapy with or without bevacizumab) in BRAF V600E-mutant metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). BREAKWATER previously met one of its dual primary endpoints, objective response rate by blinded independent central review (60.9% vs. 40.0%, OR=2.443, P=0.0008), leading to accelerated FDA approval.

METHODS: Patients with previously untreated BRAF V600E-mutant mCRC were randomized to receive EC (n=158), EC+mFOLFOX6 (n=236) or SOC (n=243). The dual primary endpoints were objective response rate and progression-free survival by BICR. The key secondary endpoint was overall survival.

RESULTS: BREAKWATER met its other dual primary endpoint, demonstrating significant PFS improvement with EC+mFOLFOX6 vs. SOC: HR 0.53 (95% CI 0.407–0.677; P<0.0001); median PFS 12.8 vs. 7.1 months. Interim analysis of OS demonstrated significant improvement: HR 0.49 (95% CI 0.375–0.632; P<0.0001); median OS 30.3 vs. 15.1 months.

Study Design

BREAKWATER (NCT04607421) is a phase 3 study evaluating EC with or without mFOLFOX6 vs investigator's choice of chemotherapy ± bevacizumab in patients with previously untreated BRAF V600E-mutant mCRC. Patients were randomized 1:1:1. Enrolled between November 2021 and December 2023 in 28 countries.

Inclusion: ≥16 years, Stage IV mCRC, BRAF V600E mutation, ECOG PS 0–1, no prior systemic therapy for metastatic disease.

Exclusion: Prior BRAF/EGFR inhibitors, symptomatic brain metastases, MSI-H/dMMR (unless ICI-ineligible), RAS mutation.

Safety

Grade 3/4 AEs: EC+mFOLFOX6 81.5% vs SOC 66.8%. Grade 5 AEs: 4.3% vs 4.4%. Serious AEs: 46.1% vs 38.9%. AEs leading to discontinuation of any drug: 26.7% vs 17.5%. Safety profile consistent with known profiles of each agent.

Subsequent Therapies & PFS2

Among patients who discontinued, 63.9% (EC+mFOLFOX6) and 61.2% (SOC) received subsequent therapy. In the SOC arm, 71.9% received subsequent BRAF inhibitor-based therapy. Median PFS2: EC+mFOLFOX6 20.7 months vs SOC 12.7 months.

Conclusions

BREAKWATER demonstrated statistically significant improvements in PFS and OS with first-line EC+mFOLFOX6 vs SOC in patients with BRAF V600E-mutant mCRC. Median OS of 30.3 months is similar to BRAF wild-type mCRC, despite historically poor prognosis. EC+mFOLFOX6 was granted accelerated approval by the US FDA as part of Project FrontRunner.
