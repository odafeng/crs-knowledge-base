Encorafenib, Binimetinib, and Cetuximab in BRAF V600E-Mutated Colorectal Cancer

Authors: Kopetz S, Grothey A, Yaeger R, et al.
Journal: N Engl J Med 2019;381:1632-1643
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1908075
PMID: 31566309
Study: BEACON CRC (Phase 3)
Note: Full text for this article is not available in PubMed Central (PMC). Full text is available at https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa1908075.

Abstract

Design: Phase 3 randomized trial enrolling 665 patients with BRAF V600E-mutant metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) who had progressed after 1-2 prior regimens in the metastatic setting.

Arms: Patients were randomized 1:1:1 to:

  • Triplet: encorafenib + binimetinib + cetuximab
  • Doublet: encorafenib + cetuximab
  • Control: cetuximab + irinotecan or FOLFIRI

Key Results (Interim Analysis)

Endpoint Triplet Doublet Control
Median OS 9.0 months 8.4 months 5.4 months
OS HR vs Control 0.52 (P<0.001) 0.60 --
ORR 26% -- 2%
ORR P-value P<0.001 (Triplet vs Control)
Grade ≥3 AEs 58% 50% 61%

Conclusions

The triplet combination of encorafenib, binimetinib, and cetuximab, as well as the doublet of encorafenib plus cetuximab, resulted in significantly longer overall survival and a higher response rate than standard therapy in patients with BRAF V600E-mutant mCRC. This was the first demonstration of survival benefit for a chemotherapy-free targeted regimen in biomarker-selected patients with mCRC.
