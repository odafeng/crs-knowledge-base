Circulating Tumor (ct)DNA Analysis of BRAF V600E Dynamics and Changes in Genomic Landscape in Patients with First-Line BRAF V600E-Mutant mCRC Treated in BREAKWATER

Authors: Kopetz S, Shah K, Yoshino T, Yaeger R, Eng C, Van Cutsem E, et al.
Presented at: ESMO 2025 (Oral Presentation)
Study: BREAKWATER (Phase 3)
Note: This is a conference presentation. No peer-reviewed journal full text is available.

Key Findings

ctDNA vs Tissue Concordance

Concordance between ctDNA-based and tissue-based BRAF V600E detection was 87.4%.

Baseline BRAF V600E VAF and Outcomes

EC+mFOLFOX6 demonstrated ORR and OS benefit independent of baseline BRAF V600E variant allele frequency (VAF), indicating that the treatment effect was consistent regardless of baseline ctDNA levels.

ctDNA Dynamics at Cycle 2, Day 15 (C2D15)

Detected-to-Not Detected (D/ND) conversion rates at C2D15 by treatment arm:

Arm D/ND Conversion Rate
EC+mFOLFOX6 67.1%
EC alone 45.2%
Control (SOC) 37.5%

Prognostic Value of ctDNA Clearance

Patients who achieved D/ND conversion (ctDNA clearance) had substantially improved outcomes:

  • Median OS = Not Estimable (NE) for D/ND patients
  • HR 0.30 vs patients who remained D/D (detected/detected)

Resistance Mutations at Cycle 7, Day 1 (C7D1)

Emergence of resistance mutations differed significantly by treatment arm:

Arm Resistance Mutations at C7D1
EC+mFOLFOX6 6.6%
EC alone 37.9%

These findings suggest that the addition of chemotherapy to EC significantly delays the emergence of resistance mutations, potentially explaining the superior duration of response and survival seen with the triplet combination.
