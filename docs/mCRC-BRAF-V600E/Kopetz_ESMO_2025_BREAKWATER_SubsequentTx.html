Subsequent Therapies in the Phase 3 BREAKWATER Study of First-Line EC + mFOLFOX6 in Patients with BRAF V600E-Mutant mCRC

Authors: Kopetz S, et al.
Presented at: ESMO 2025 (Poster)
Study: BREAKWATER (Phase 3)
Note: This is a conference poster presentation. No peer-reviewed journal full text is available.

Key Findings

Subsequent Therapy in the Control Arm

  • 71.9% of control arm patients received a subsequent BRAF inhibitor-based therapy
  • 55.4% of control arm patients received encorafenib + cetuximab (EC) as subsequent therapy

PFS2 (Progression-Free Survival After Next Line of Therapy)

Measure EC+mFOLFOX6 (1L) Control → EC (1L→2L)
Median PFS2 20.7 months 12.9 months

These data demonstrate that first-line use of EC+mFOLFOX6 results in superior cumulative disease control compared to the strategy of using chemotherapy first and reserving EC for the second line.

Surgical Conversion

  • Surgical conversion rate with EC+mFOLFOX6: 5.2%
  • Complete resection rate: 3.0%
